// THE QUESTION

Does the signal beat the benchmark?

A moving-average crossover is one of the most-cited strategies in trading — and one of the most over-claimed. This project tests it properly: compute a fast and a slow EMA, go long when the fast crosses above the slow, exit when it crosses back below — then measure the result against a plain buy & hold over the exact same window.

No survivorship tricks, no curve-fitting in disguise. Just the equity curve, the drawdowns, and the numbers that matter.

POSITION · ALWAYS IN

The takeaway isn't "the strategy wins" — it's that the crossover meaningfully cut drawdown by sitting out the worst stretches, at the cost of lag on sharp recoveries. Exactly the kind of trade-off a backtest is meant to expose.
